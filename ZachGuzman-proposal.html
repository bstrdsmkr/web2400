The SOAPIFF site is in need of a both a technology upgrade and maintenance repair. In addition to the repairs and upgrades identified in the initial analysis, the organization, and more importantly it’s customers, will benefit greatly from a sustained community engagement effort. This community engagement will be hallmarked by refocusing the site on attracting and maintaining an active community of both producers and consumers of content. Creative Solution

Zach Guzman

Look and feel

The SOAPIFF has built a unique visual identity which will be maintained in order to preserve brand recognition and community momentum. The tone of the site will be professional and creative to reflect the talent of the artitsts involved in SOAPIFF’s community.

Zach Guzman

Style guide

  • The site will use Poiret One provided by Google Fonts as the main typeface.
    • Sample text written in the Poiret One font.
  • The color palette for the site shall remain Orange #f7722d, Blue #002d56, and Grey #939598 using variations on those colors for emphasis and accentuation.
  • Background images for headers and banners will be reduced to their minimum tileable sizes and repeated in CSS in order to reduce page load time.

Zach Guzman

Layout

  • The site will be moved from a one page accordian layout into distinctive sections in order to reduce page load time and focus the site on specific content in each section.
  • Navigation for the site shall be provided through a top-mounted nav bar, collapsing to a single menu button preserve screen real estate on smaller devices.
  • The front page shall be redesigned to deliver a concise first impression to user which quickly and clearly conveys the site’s purpose
  • The site’s “Archives/Winners” section will be split off into a dedicated page and designed to provide links to the entrant’s work and capitalize on goodwill built through link sharing.
  • The site’s “Donate” section will be split off into a dedicated page designed to make the donation process easy and provide more information about the usage of donation funds and SOAPIFF’s operational costs.

Content Solution

Content sourcing

  • New images shall be provided by SOAPIFF in the following acceptable formats in descending order of preference:
    • .ps
    • .eps
    • .svg
    • .png
    • .jpg
  • Verbiage and text documents shall be exchanged in .txt format.
  • The logo, and navigation backgrounds, may be sourced from the original site if the original templates are not availabel

Zach Guzman

Page focus

The accordian sections from the front page will be broken up into separate sections in order to focus the message of each section.

  • The front page will focus on introducing the site and providing users with a good first impression.
  • The donations page will be focused on showing users why they should donate and outlining the doation process.
  • The “Archives/Winners” section will be refocused on celebrating the work of the artists involved and encouraging both new and existing artists and users to become a more active part of the SOAPIFF community.

Michael Tomlinson

Content updates

SOAPIFF will provide a link, preview image, and brief description of each entry currently listed in the “Awards/Winners” section. This content will be used to create the new Artists page showcasing the work of past entrants.

Management Solution

Zach Guzman

Scheduling

The Project schedule (included below) shall be the sole source of authority for deadlines and project tasks. Any task not listed on the schedule shall not be considered part of the project. Any incremental tasks to be added to the schedule will necessatate rearranging the schedule to account for the additional resource expenditure estimation for the new task. Scheduling will be re-evaluated upon completion of each phase and client approval will be sought for any necessary changes.

Zach Guzman

Asset storage

All assests shall be stored in a git version control repository to allow commenting as well as maintaining revision history and cryptographic integrity.

Michael Tomlinson

Approval process

All approvals shall be based on the SHA-256 hash of the relevant git commit. Approvals require 2 party consent and may be communicated via email.

Michael Tomlinson

Project delays

All time-lost delays due to content delays on the part of SOAPIFF shall be billed at 1.5x the agreed upon rate until a new schedule has been approved. The schedule includes buffer time for miscalculations and unanticipated events. Any delays on the part of the Project Manager shall be billed at 0.5x the agreed upon rate.
